# Identity-Gated Tokenized Equity Pilot One-Pager

## Summary

TAP enables a bank, broker, transfer agent, or consortium to operate a permissioned tokenized equity or fund product using private identity, suitability, and holdings verification instead of shallow wallet allowlisting. This creates a stronger operating model for regulated tokenized securities.

## What This Solves

- protects investor identity and suitability data
- links transfer restrictions to deterministic policy state
- supports mixed integration across APIs and zkTLS-attested HTTPS sources

## Pilot Scope

- product: permissioned tokenized equity or fund
- sources: identity and KYC provider, suitability source, custody holdings API
- controls: investor eligibility proofs and transfer compliance proofs
- deployment: self-hosted, private-rollup ready

## Why It Matters

Public tokenized securities are usually limited by coarse gating and weak privacy. TAP demonstrates how a regulated institution can operate tokenized securities with stronger investor protections, richer compliance controls, and better consortium infrastructure ownership.

## What the Pilot Demonstrates

- private investor eligibility checks
- policy-linked access and transfer control
- mixed adapter and zkTLS integration support
- auditable proof and settlement artifacts

## Deliverables

- configured tenant and provider integrations
- policy version 1 for investor eligibility and transfer restriction
- source collect transcripts for identity and holdings
- zkTLS transcript for suitability
- transfer compliance transcript
- redacted demo pack and release bundle

## Expansion Path

- issuer allocation workflows
- multi-jurisdiction rule packs
- transfer-agent and fund-admin integrations
- consortium-operated tokenized market infrastructure
